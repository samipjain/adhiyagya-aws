Ab hum bhi English bolenge

With over 100 children at our first center, Adhiyagya decided to spread its wings and bring more children under its purview in order to empower them and bring a difference into their lives through Education.
Mr. Arun Goyal retired as General Manager IDBI, has always been actively involved in social work and is a part of the management team at Adhiyagya. Under his support and guidance, the second center at Sector 45, Faridabad was set up on 28th November 2015.
On interaction with the children and their parents living in the surroundings of Sector 45, Faridabad we understood that while some children were enrolled into nearby schools many of them did not go to school. However, even the school going children were lacking far behind in the very basics. For us it was an opportunity to fulfill our mission of bridging this gap therefore we decided to undertake this task as a challenge to bring about a positive change in the lives of these children.
The thought of being educated brought a new level of excitement among the children and they were keen to begin with the classes at the earliest. The first class was conducted on 28th November 2015 and was attended by 13 children. The word soon got around and the number of children started increasing. At present the number of children is over 35 and is consistently increasing. The classes are conducted in small batches, twice over the weekend, with the help of 7 volunteers. Our volunteer Apoorva Gupta, who is an Engineering student, is actively managing the center.
The children are being taught as per Adhiyagya’s curriculum drafted under the project – “Ab hum bhi English bolenge” to focus on building vocabulary, improving reading and writing skills and teaching the children Basic English grammar. However, we also felt the need to impart moral education to the children since many of them were not attending school and at this impressionable age it was very important to imbibe good values in them. Hence, special focus is laid on this aspect and initiatives such as value based story telling and innovative ways of teaching good habits to the children have been adopted.
It is our endeavor to inculcate in them the confidence and to provide them an environment that encourages them to learn, a privilege that they have been so unfairly deprived of.
