Diwali Celebrations – 8th November 2015

Because Diwali is all about winning over darkness
Diwali is called the festival of lights. It is defined as the power to overcome the evil. Even when the night is dark with no moonlight, there is hope that lights up the world on this day. With this thought in the hearts, Adhiyagya celebrated Diwali with 90 kids at Rajasthani Camp, Sarita Vihar on 8th November 2015.
The volunteers from ‘Igniting Young Minds’ (IYM) of Leaders of Tomorrow (Delhi University Organization) became part of the celebrations.There were 9 volunteers from IYM. Each volunteer engaged small groups of children with fun activities. Some danced, some sang and others played games as Diwali is all about spreading joy around.
Keeping up with the tradition of cleaning our homes before Diwali all the children at Adhiyagya with the help of volunteers first cleaned the center and it’s surrounding. Thereafter all the kids decorated the center beautifully. Every child was given a diya each to decorate and thus there were 90 diyas all decorated in their own elegant style, which were then lighted on that day. Decorations are incomplete without rangolis. The place was filled with colorful patterns made by the older girls.
It becomes necessary at times to inform children about the true and right spirit of Diwali, especially with young kids. It should not be just an occasion to burn crackers therefore; the students of class 11th and 12th enlightened everybody about Diwali. Thereafter, we had Ganesh-Lakshmi puja and sweets (prasad) & refreshments were distributed amongst kids.
Diwali was celebrated with full zest with a shine and smile on their faces.
