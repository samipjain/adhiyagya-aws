Annual Event 2017 – Carnival & Exhibition

Adhiyagya Turns 2
On February 5, 2017, team Adhiyagya came together to celebrate 2 years of its birth. The Annual Event was a celebration to reminisce the beautiful journey of Adhiyagya which began from merely 12 students at one centre and now has more than 200 students and 2 centers.
The zeal of the children was unbeatable; they all looked smart in their brand new Adhiyagya T-Shirts and their sparkling eyes depicted mixed emotions of nervousness and excitement.
The programme was kick started by the National Anthem followed by the song adopted by Adhiyagya – Desh Hume Deta Hai Sab Kuch which was sung by all the children and volunteers.
This year’s annual event’s theme was ‘carnival cum exhibition’. The theme was selected to supplement the mundane classroom teaching with practical training, so that the children gain working knowledge of the concepts that they only read in textbooks.
The children of Adhiyagya had exhibited various impeccable models for the visitors displaying basic concepts of general science and mathematics, ranging from a model volcano, respiratory system, kaleidoscope, Pythagoras theorem to color wheel showing VIBGYOR colours turning into white colour. This experience helped the children to get a practical understanding of those concepts of science which earlier seemed incomprehensible to these young minds. All the models were built by the children on their own which helped them to appreciate these concepts better and taught them to apply these concepts in their daily life.
Apart from this, the volunteers had also put up the following games stalls, in order to keep the visitors and children entertained, these included, snakes and ladders with a twist, minute by minute (which required the participants to finish fun tasks within a minute), hoopla, tattoo stall etc. Additionally, few residents of Sarita Vihar and students from Enactus, Institute of Home Economics, Delhi University has also put up games stalls in order to encourage and help the children.
In addition to the above, the children had prepared certain educational skits which taught them the table of 9, the concept of fractions, the concept of acids and bases and the importance of nutrients in our food.
The students of Adhiyagya, since the very beginning have always shown immense interest and inclination towards music and dance, and our annual event would have been incomplete without any musical intervention. Accordingly, a dance workshop was conducted by Moving Souls for the children. Apart from the kids of Adhiyagya, several visitors and volunteers also enjoyed the dance workshop. Thereafter, IGNIA a sufi band based in Delhi, was invited to grace the occasion and they gave a fabulously melodious performance and made everyone sing to their tunes.
As they say all work and no play makes jack a dull boy, and thus in order to supplement this educational journey with yet another play element, the students performed a flash mob. This performance was truly scintillating and left the crowd asking for more.
With this, the event came to an end, but just before we parted, Adhiyagya presented each of its students with a mug of Adhiyagya and the volunteers with certificates, in order to value their dedication as without their dedication and spirit, the organization wouldn’t have reached where it is today.
