Republic Day Celebrations – 24th January 2016

At Sector 45, Faridabad
India celebrated its 67th Republic Day on January 26, 2016. As this day is celebrated with great enthusiasm, so did we at Adhiyagya celebrate it at our center in Faridabad on January 24th. The day began with the voices of children saying ‘Jai Hind, Jai Bharat’ reverberating in the air. Volunteers of each batch prepared the children for their respective performances. Our youngest kids went first to recite a poem with actions. They also danced on the song ‘Bam Bam Boley’ from the movie – Taare Zameen Par. The next group performed a parade and learned how to march with the positions like ‘stand at ease’ and ‘attention’ and the right way to salute. The others enlightened everyone about Republic Day, our national flag – Tiranga and all other national symbols. Some children disguised themselves as freedom fighters and gave sensational performances. The children were bestowed with the blessings from Mr. Arun Goyal who taught them about the courage and heroic act of Guru Gobind Singh’s sons. All the children made flags for themselves each unique in its own way. The spirit of nationalism filled the surroundings and also the heart of each one present there.

At Rajasthani Camp, Sarita Vihar
Adhiyagya celebrated Republic Day at its Rajasthani Camp, Sarita Vihar center on 24th January 2016 with full patriotic fervor. As it was a Sunday, the volunteers from Delhi University had come over for their session with the children. The children were taught about India, its national symbols, current affairs, importance of Republic Day etc. a week in advance. The children were asked to gear up for a round of quiz and a public speaking competition. To begin with, the groups had an internal public speaking competition followed by an inter-group quiz competition. Thereafter all the children settled outside for the main celebration. Each group had prepared some patriotic and motivational songs and the groups came forward one by one, sang the songs and charged the atmosphere. The winners from all groups were applauded and prizes were given to encourage the children. All the volunteers and children came together to sing ‘Hum honge Kamyaab’ while waving the national flags. The celebrations ended with the National Anthem and salute to the nation amidst the chorus of ‘Vande Mataram’ and ‘Jai Hind’.
