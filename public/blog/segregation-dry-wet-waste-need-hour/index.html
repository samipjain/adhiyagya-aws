  • Segregation of Wet & Dry Waste – Need of the Hour
    Posted Date December 23, 2016

    The life of any human being has been so evolved that it is almost impossible to imagine a day in life without a human generating waste as a part of anyone’s daily chores. However, we are also aware of the harmful effects of such waste materials and their impact on the environment and human health if they aren’t treated in the right manner. One solution that slowly people have begun to embrace is by diligently making use of garbage bins rather than otherwise littering public places.

    However, is it enough to make sure that the garbage reaches the bin? Based on various researches, setting aside dry waste and wet waste is one of leading way in which waste management is in existence. Hence, throwing garbage in the bin, is not where the process of managing waste ends, but where it begins. Segregation is the first step of waste management and it’s something we must follow scrupulously. While most people think that it starts once the garbage reaches the huge dustbin vans or the dumping grounds, but it really starts right at our house. Separating our waste is essential as the amount of waste generated today causes immense problem.

    Household waste should be segregated which means dividing waste into dry
    and wet. On one hand the Dry waste includes wood products, metals, glass etc. Whereas, on the other hand the wet waste, typically refers to organic waste which usually are heavy in weight due to dampness. The dry and wet waste needs to be separated so that the dry waste can be recycled whereas the wet waste can be composted. Segregation of waste is the need of the hour as it will help in reduction of waste that reaches the landfill and will further help in the reduction of air and water pollution.

    Measures should be taken by the government to make people aware about this. We at Adhiyagya are taking a small initiative to educate and make people aware about the importance of cleanliness and help in contributing to the Swachh Bharat Abhiyaan.

    Following are the steps to be practiced at home that would help in the segregation of dry and wet waste:
    1. Keep separate containers for dry and wet waste in the kitchen.
    2. Keep two bags for waste; one for collection of dry waste such as paper and plastic and the other for the rest of the household waste
    3. Keep plastic from the kitchen clean and dry and drop into the dry waste bin.
    4. Keep glass/plastic containers rinsed of food matter.
    5. Send wet waste out of the home daily.
    6. Store and send dry waste out of the home once a week.
    7. Keep a separate paper bag for throwing the sanitary
    waste.

    Being aware and responsible is the best way of protecting ourselves and our environment from the toxic effects of such waste. If each of us takes this responsibility we can make a better and a cleaner India.

    – Samridhi Sharma
