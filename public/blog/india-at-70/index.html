  • India at 70 - Education
    Posted Date August 31, 2017

    As Independent India turns 70, it is time to rejoice and look back at how much we have achieved in all these years. While there has been progress in many fronts but somewhere our growth story hasn't been exceptional. We take pride in the values that our culturally rich country stands for however we are shackled by various evils such as casteism, appeasement politics, superstitions etc. I believe that an educated society has the wisdom to overpower these evils and move towards development.

    Education for all -This constitutional right is still a faraway dream for majority of Indian children. We come from a society where children do not have the access to basic rights such as food, clothing, shelter and safety. Hence education becomes an unimportant, privileged right. However, it can be a solution to most of the problems faced by us in India. Education has the power to bring an end to the vicious circle of poverty. But for that the focus needs to be on ‘Education for all’. Education is not a luxury to be afforded by the rich; it is the right of every child in this country. To ensure this change it is essential to create opportunity. It is essential to create hopes and dreams. It is essential to create awareness. It is also essential to break all socioeconomic barriers that have formed an adverse outlook in the minds of people since ages. It is time we consider Education as a compulsion where the receiver does not face any discrimination. No educated person ever let’s his children go uneducated because he realizes the importance and the difference that education has made to his life. Therefore in my view, the task at hand is to educate one generation of each family and that will ensure cessation of illiteracy from that family.

    Over the years the education system in government schools has deteriorated continuously. The present condition of schools is pitiful with poor infrastructure and lack of basic amenities, classrooms full of children with barely any place to sit, teachers who come not to teach but to ensure that they get their paycheck and children who come not to learn but because they are forced to or in some cases for the free meals. Overall the schools are no longer temples of knowledge, and discipline and education fall far behind.

    Adhiyagya was started with the aim of providing quality education to the children from the economically weaker sections of the society, in order to empower them and to create opportunities for them to live a self-made dignified life. Adhiyagya focuses on four core principles – Education, Health & Hygiene, Nationalism and Moral Values. Our efforts are concentrated to bridge the gap between the ‘what should be taught’ and ‘what is taught’ by providing quality education through our projects imparting English, Mathematics & Computer education along with special emphasis on moral values and discipline. The organization also works towards the wholesome development of the children and therefore many events and activities are organized wherein the children are provided a platform to showcase their talent that helps boost their self-confidence.

    To truly bring about a difference, the education system needs an overhaul. It is important that the schools provide quality education that each child deserves, as guaranteed by our constitution. It is time where the specific needs of various segments are understood and appropriate methods be used. The focus needs to be shifted on providing basic education where the schools, teachers, parents and children work together in a systematic manner to ensure the transmission of knowledge. Our country can become unstoppable if we are able to convert our youth into an educated & skilled workforce. I strongly believe that, as a society, it should be our endeavor to help children come out of the shackles of illiteracy and ignorance by guiding them through the journey of gaining knowledge.

    – Kunjal Sehgal
    She is a chartered accountant who left her corporate job and started Adhiyagya in January 2015. During the last two and a half years the organization has impacted the lives of over 200 children from its two centres in New Delhi and Faridabad with a team of over 40 volunteers.
