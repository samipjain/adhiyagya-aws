Computer ki Paathshala

Computers have become an indispensible part of our lives today. Now that we see the use of computers in all spheres of life, its education is very important in getting good jobs. In order to empower the children at Adhiyagya, another project was started to impart computer education. To begin with two computer machines were installed at our Rajasthani Camp Center with support of Mrs. Rekha Mukhija and Mr V.K Sehgal. The project focuses at getting the children acquainted with the Basics of computer and MS-Office. The aim of this project is to ensure that basic computer education is imparted to the children to instill in them self-assurance that they do not lag behind their counterparts in any way. We also want to ensure that lack of computer education doesn’t become a hurdle for the children when they begin to seek jobs.
While we were planning to begin this project, Samip Jain and Niraj Gupta approached us. They wanted to get involved and contribute to the cause of Adhiyagya. Both of them, being Software developers, helped design a curriculum that would prove beneficial to the children. We decided to begin with our 11th & 12th class students. The first class was held on 21st November 2015. The instant reaction of the children was heartening and we realized that it was something that they were all very keen to learn. It was evident that they knew the importance of computer education.
Simultaneously, Mrs. Rekha Mukhija began computer education for another batch in the morning. The curriculum followed is the same involving Basics of computer & MS-Office. Therefore we have close to 25 children who are learning computer at present. The aim is to educate these children and encourage them to teach the same to the younger ones to create a chain of learning among the children within the organization.
The children are free to use computers while the classes are not going on. The freedom to work on computers has instilled a lot of enthusiasm among the children. They daily spend time trying to learn some new aspect about the machines that were for so long kept away from them.

“We came across Adhiyagya through social network and got in touch with them. Amazed by their vision of empowering children with basic skills necessary to be able to build a better future, we were eager to help. In the short span of time, that we have been associated with Adhiyagya, we were able to see the eagerness in these kids to learn and bring positive change to their lives. This has motivated us even more and we hope that we will be able to help them in every possible way that we can. Indeed, it is true that to be able to help and empower others is satisfying.”
– Samip Jain & Niraj Gupta
