Fun & Fitness!

“A sound mind in a sound body is a short but full description of a happy state in this world.”
– John Locke

Health is real wealth. A healthy person is an asset to himself, to his family and to his community. Good Health starts from the very infancy. It is here that maximum protection and care is neededbut often the health of children remains neglected andthis affects their education as well.

In order to achieve our mission of providing quality education it is essential that the children are in the best of their health and therefore we initiated another project – ‘Fun & Fitness!’ focusing on the well being of children.

The ‘Fun & Fitness!’ sessions are held on every Sunday morning at the L-block Park in Sarita Vihar, 6 am onwards. The children spend close to 3 hours in the open air, exercising and having fun. The first session was held on 23rd August 2015. The children have been so thrilled with these sessions that they wake up early on Sunday mornings (their only holiday!) and are all waiting eagerly at the park for the session to begin.

The entire session has been divided into three parts that involves yoga, racing and group games. Mr. Ramesh Nichani, a resident of C-block, Sarita Vihar, conducts the Yoga session. He has retired as GM (Finance) from WAPCOS. He is actively involved in various community activities and has strongly taken up the cause of yoga and naturopathy. The children practice yoga asanas for one hour. It involves SukshmaKriya, various asanas followed by Pranayam. Yoga is a healthy practice that helps create a balance between the body, mind and soul. Our aim is to encourage children to understand its benefits and to practice yoga in their daily lives so as to reap maximum benefits for their health.

Running is also a healthy exercise and therefore the yoga session is followed by racing. While the children enjoy it the most it also helps to build healthy competition among them. Thereafter the children are divided into teams and group games such as Kho-Kho, Dog and the bone, Fire on the mountain etc are played. These games are not only fun but also help in inculcating the virtues of team spirit and sportsmanship among the children. This session is a fun filled platform where children of all age groups come together to play and create a bonding.

“I enjoy teaching Yoga to children of Adhiyagya. Children are smart, responsible and intelligent. They take active interest in learning Yoga and could also be groomed as Yoga teachers.”
– Ramesh Nichani
